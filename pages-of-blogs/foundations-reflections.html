⭒ Foundations Reflections ⭒

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I found out that writing essays is a lovely way to let your brain breath after you’ve been (eye)balls deep in code for a few days.

If we’re talking about the ego that takes a beating when you lose a battle or your self worth is challenged, I think that ego did OK because I never had a chance to compare my progress to anybody else's. Initially I was finding it really hard to have any sense of progress without having peers or feedback as a reference point, but slowly I grew to to really like it. I learnt to take satisfaction from my own personal and learned to appreciate feeling like the work I was doing was self motivated and not motivated by competitiveness. No tall poppies, no comparison thieving joy. Just good hard work. So I guess I learnt constant feedback isn’t so important while you’re learning. Everybody messes up the first few pancakes and that’s fine so long as you’re making progress and upskilling.

I learnt that I can really focus on task if I need to and all I really need to do is build a strong base programme and then the dopamine kicks in and I can go for hours. As soon as I start having fun with a project, and feeling like I can make it do whatever I want, the deep focus kicks in and it’s great.

I also learned studying learning is a really awesome motivating way to get better at learning - I'm a sucker for a cool weird new memory technique or a kooky learning twist that makes everything better, so learning how to learn about learning was a really big bonus for me.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values shape how you act and control yourself in professional settings and work environments particularly when you practise mindfulness (the act of triaging emotion on the way in and delving into the root of why you’re feeling them and how you should refactor them before you express them outwardly) Your values will be key in assessing why you do this, and will help you hown this skill and use it for the greatest possible good if you are willing to practise.

Do you value stability and diligence in yourself? Mindfulness will help you listen to these values and control frustration during problem solving struggles.

Do you value achievement and perseverance? Mindfulness will help cull defeatest self-talk and negative ideation.

Empathy impacts how you interact with the people you share your learning environment with- it changes how you process other peoples actions and allows your reaction to them to be geared towards the best possible outcome, having mutual respect and a moral sense of human understanding. Its the acknowledgement that we are all complex imperfect beings, we all have our perspective shaped by unique circumstances and we all have the right to be heard and treated with respect.

Self awareness makes both these value based behaviour filters much easier to manage and regulate. Without being aware of your flaws and thought processing sequences it would be really hard to change them for the better.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I found the research more interesting than I thought it would be. I started applying the techniques early on and reaching for different resources that I never got to use for my old uni essays (ted talks, podcasts) and it made writing them really fun. Im pretty self indulgent when it comes to yabbering on about nothing so that was nice. Not having to reference or write to structure is lovely.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Trying to find personal examples derived from experiences I haven't had yet in order to meet unit standards.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Human skills are as important as tech skills in this industry. Even more so now people are moving back into the office and AI is changing how people get hired. They help shape the learning proccess and the earlier they are learned and reinforced with reflection the more helpful and permanent the learning will be. It also offers a pleasant alternative to the tech work, gives that learning time to settle while we get a break, but also stay present and learning and keeping up with our commitments to maintaining nuero-plasticity.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time?

Sometimes there are subjects that we spend too long on that could be made simpler. For instance I think EQ vs IQ can be summed up in a few paragraphs. Not a few hours. I feel like we could cut half of some of the more bumbling essays and maybe study industry structure and jargon instead. Or debugging. Or how to integrate AI into our learning. Or particularly sticky bits of coding technique that would make more sense with a little delving.

Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Core and tech both need to be respected. I generally switch to core because I feel like a having a break from code but I don’t want to stop. If I stayed programing through that time instead it would be bad for my brain and bad for my code. I want to gain as many skills as possible while I’m training and neglecting half the training matter would really hinder that. Plus you never know what useful rabbit hole you can find until you go looking. There is time for everything.
