⭒ IQ vs EQ ⭒


Intelligence quotient treats the human mind like a calculator - it is a quantifiable rating that reflects how well you can use logic and reason to solve problems.


It used to be sort of a big deal - back before we had smartphones as our built-in memory drives, the ability to spit out facts and do math used to mean something. But now we keep out android tech comfortably installed in our pockets there's a new intelligence rising, and it’s something our phones can’t do for us.

Emotional intelligence is a powerful skill. It allows us to consider our emotional response to stimuli separately, before we react to it, and choose to force our reactions through a different filter if our initial reaction doesn’t serve us.

It makes us resilient, compassionate, pragmatic and level headed, and combined with a solid IQ becomes a very very potent social tool.


IQ is quantifiable and covers learnt skills like mathematics, spatial reasoning, recall and logic, but as technology clears time as space for us to perform higher learning it has become far less powerful than the social grace gifted to us by emotional intelligence.

EQ spans a much deeper understanding of the human condition, it teaches us new levels of patience, analytical understanding of ourselves and others and gives us far greater control over our communication and actions.


At its root EQ allows us to recognise rogue emotion and helps us navigate it so we can achieve a clearer decision making process and engage in communication shaped with empathy and human understanding. This self regulation and self awareness leads us towards a cleaner mental function and better decision making.


It seems people often mistake EQ for something that is emotionally driven - it is really using logic to recognise and reorder emotion as it occurs and judge weither or not it is serving our decision making process at the time.


When I was learning to meditate one of the key practises starting out was learning how to deal with visiting emotion during sessions (which should be clear, empty time, but its rarely perfect - nobodies perfect, its OK) in quiet moments of stilling the mind we learnt to acknowledge emotion and thoughts as they rose but then to trust ourselves that they served no purpose and let them go immediately.

This acknowledgement and self acceptance gives you a deeper sense of peace and helps you trust yourself more and get even better a stilling your mind.


When you feel the anger bubble up inside of you because someone left the door open when they left the flat last night and the lounge is now a balmy 6 degrees celcius this morning, take a moment.

You have several paths you could go down from here.


Path one: listen to your adrenal system, the path where its 7am, you haven’t had coffee and you’re propper pissed off:

The immediate anger and stress fills you with raging epinephrine, cortisol, and norepinephrine, your blood pressure rises, you heart rate quickens, your body spikes its need for sugars and your flight or fight nodes switch on as your lizard brain readies itself for combat.

Even though it only takes a few minutes to process the adrenaline and a few hours to work through the cortisol if anything else stresses you before your body and hormone levels return to normal your levels will spike back up to prime stress.

You’ve just let your flatmate ruin your day and if you go dip into that coffee its going to smash your hormones even more.

Without emotional regulation you stay mad, you get stuck in traffic on the way to work and end up staying pissed off until you put yourself with a food coma at lunch and chill.

When your body is in alarm phase it puts loads of vital processing on hold - you burn through extra oxygen, and any energy being directed to recovery and healing is repurposed and used for your stress response.

You feel pain more intensely and any conditions or ailments you have get worse - migraines, muscular issues, old injuries, back pain, the blood sugar disruptions inhibit your immune systems and wreck your appetite, sending you into a deep sugar craving mode, or a fasting state to keep your more agile and mentally aware.

The blood pressure fluctuations effect your tissues and heart. And the hormone fluctuations make you a real dickhead to be around.


Path two: recognise your adrenal system is about to kick off, because its 7am and you haven’t had coffee, put the heat pump on blast and treat yourself to a hot shower.

I won’t write a story but you can leave the door open if you want and maliciously freeze the bastard out, calmy, comfortably, knowing karma is a good bitch and you’re going to have a lovely day.

Or you can leave the heat pump on and he buys you a beer after work to say sorry.

Either way, hormonally and karmically speaking, you just won.


Practising EQ during everyday life teaches us to do this with all emotions, we greet them when they show up, check what they’re going to do and process them effectively - we learn our triggers, we learn to know what emotions will trigger in us and learn to think ahead a few steps and decide whether or not they serve us before we process them.

Eventually we learn how to perform the same emotional dissection on others (empathy might be a nicer word for that) using compassion as we’re analysing their actions, and our reactions before we proceed with interacting.


Amongst many things this helps regulate unnecessary stress, replace split second judgements with empathy and pragmatism and generally be more open to alternate interpretations of the situations we find ourselves instead of just following the chemical roller-coaster that our flight and sight systems can send us on.

As someone that used to be particularly ruled by my adrenal system I can particularly relate to this.


By understanding our flaws, patterns and knee jerk responses we can develop techniques and call ourselves on our own negative repeat behaviours, reinforce positive neural pathways and evolve to be better.


I recently learnt an interesting technique for imposter syndrome (and for quieting that inner critic that we’ve been asked to journal about so frequently) where in times of self doubt when the internal forces are being your enemy you single each one out and imagine is as a single entity, personify them, give them a voice. Choose the loudest, rudest, most distinct one first, and imagine it turning into a tiny ugly mouse and put it in a jar.

There’s a volume button on the jar - turn the volume right up, listen to the shrieking mouse telling you how to think, then turn it right down until its completely silent and just screaming into the ether.

Take a moment to relish their silence... Now go to the next, turn it into a mouse and continue the process until they’re all gone.

Then focus.


Its a weird one but its pretty shocking how much you can let pointless external influences effect your private inner workings when you look under the surface for a little.


There are hundreds of techniques.

Find what works for you.


EQ is exceptionally important in hierarchical work places - jobs can be taught, and a genuine interest in upskilling can be as much of a positive as already being fluent if you can show you would be a great hire.

The ability to be a level, pleasant human being that works well as part of a team is actually much harder for some people to master than the actual skills they need to do their jobs.

And some people are assholes, clever assholes, but still assholes.

People skills, networking, confidence, the ability to manage nerves, stress, pressure will get you much further in a job interview than the ability to flash cram python into your head.

So use this knowledge and remember to check yourself. And check in with yourself.

Use your IQ to boost your EQ and put your enemies in a mouse jar.
Leave the heat pump on and only drink coffee when you actually need it.

Breath.
Chill.
And don’t stress too much.


Nobodies perfect, but we can always be a little bit better if we try.
