⭒ Te Whare Tapa Whā ⭒


Te Whare Tapa Whā is a holistic approach to wellbeing incorporating taha tinana (the physical) taha hinengaro (the emotional and mental) taha wairua (the spiritual) and taha whānau ( the wellbeing of ones family)

These 4 dimensions are often represented by the 4 walls of a whare thats rests upon strong roots, the whenua, the 5th key element of this model that links environment, sense of belonging and identity, to an individuals wellness.

The following commitments constitute my wellbeing plan based on these concepts.




Taha Tinana Physical Wellbeing


I take great care of my health and wellbeing - I’m a devout yogi (at the studio 5 days a week) and schedule my life around this practise. I mostly practise very early in the morning and smash a massive breakfast and a tonne of water as soon as I get home.

I pre-plan and batch cook my meals so I know I have awesome balanced kai ready to go, have a strict nootropic and vitamin regiment that I follow and stay well away from delicious intoxicants like alcohol and coffee, mostly due to an unfortunate dopamine fast that went really well for me.




Taha Hinengaro Emotional & Mental Wellbeing


Yoga helps a lot with my state of mind.

I also follow pretty strict daily routines to keep me from letting ADHD knock me too far off track. I’ve learnt that I feel quite guilty for breaking routines, and end up way happier and healthier if I stick to them so I do.

When I wake up I make my bed and leave the house as fast as I possibly can. Natural light is amazing for mental health - I listen to a podcast on my way to yoga to wake my brain up and have a little company (I know it sounds a little sad but I get up early and theres never anyone to talk to so the entertainment really helps)

I clear my head, run through my practise, suss my daily meditation and listen to the news on my way home so I can feel a bit more connected to the world.

I socialise a lot and treat myself well. When I take breaks I always make sure its worthwhile - I always have a project to work on, or something nice to do for myself, I look after my plants, and I let myself rest a lot. I also forgive myself for resting, and for not being good at routines sometimes, because nobody's perfect and taking a cold shower everyday is actually pretty difficult in winter sometimes. Little slip ups just aren't worth the cortisol load. Forgive, eat some cheese and keep going.

I also try and encourage the people around me to be really mindful and chill as well - going for a saunas so much nicer if you’re taking a friend that needs it just as badly as you do, cooking a nice meal feels twice as good if you’re going to share it and starting a news projects even more satisfying if you’ve got someone to share the memory with.




Taha Wairua Spiritual Wellbeing


I don’t consider myself a spiritual person but I do like to feel connected to my communities.

For me spiritual wellbeing is a deep sense of peace that I get from living a whole and satisfying life and feeling like I’ve been making good choices. I nurture it by learning, growing and giving myself creative tasks that allow me to feel like I’ve bought some good energy into the world.




Taha Whānau Family Wellbeing


I don’t have much family in Wellington but I really like being around my urban tribe.

Being social and making sure my people are healthy, respected and receive the same care that they give is very important to me. Also standing with them when times aren’t so great. I extend the same forgiveness I give myself to my friends and family. Nobodies perfect, but they can still be wonderful.




Whenua Connection, Place, Belonging


I go to nature to recharge. I live in the city and this place feels so tiny sometimes - I find wide open space really great for a recharge and I’m constantly surprised by how beautiful Wellington can be. I go to the beach a lot, and I walk around Wellington all the time (because I dont have a car, but also its my guaranteed break from screens, designated podcast time and the serenity and space is so healing)

I find a massive solace in stargazing and gardening.

My sense of whenua comes from being here for half my life - I have so many special spaces I love to visit and secret tracks I can follow to visit old memories, an I always actively try to find more. I love Wellington so much I really think this is the perfect city for me.

Theres so much beauty here.
